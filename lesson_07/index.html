Childhood

Salvador Dalí was born on 11 May 1904 to notary Salvador Dalí i Cusi (1872–1952) and Felipa Domenech (1874–1921) in the town of Figueres in Catalonia , in the north-east of the Kingdom of Spain . He was born nine months after the death of his brother, also named Salvador (1901–1903). When he was five years old, his parents took him to his brother's grave and told him that he was his reincarnation . The news that he was a copy of his brother caused him to fear his brother's grave and a desire to prove his uniqueness to the world.

From early childhood, Salvador demonstrated a bright individuality, in particular through his energy and eccentricity, which manifested itself in frequent whims and tantrums. This behavior irritated his father, who reacted negatively to his son's antics, while his mother, on the contrary, always supported and forgave him. As a result, in Salvador's mind, his father became a symbol of severity and evil, and his mother - the personification of goodness.

Salvador's talent for drawing appeared at a fairly young age. Already at the age of four, he, with a diligence that was surprising for such a young child, tried to draw. Salvador Dali painted his first painting when he was six years old. It was a small impressionistic landscape , painted with oil paints on a wooden board.

Dali would sit in a small, specially designed room for this purpose for whole days, drawing pictures. In the town of Figueres , Dali took drawing lessons from Professor Joan Núñez. Among the favorite paintings of the young Dali was the composition of the French artist Millet "Angelus" (the first word of the prayer "Angelus Domini", which means "Angel of the Lord"), which depicts a scene of the evening prayer of potato pickers .
